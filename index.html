    • Johann Rupert: A tremendous commitment to nurturing values and society’s increased value of sport

    December 27, 2019December 27, 2019| Minnie ZookMinnie Zook| 0 Comment
    Johann Rupert is a businessman from South Africa who grew up in Stellenbosch and started his banking career in New York City. He went back to South Africa in 1979 and started the Rand Merchant Bank, which he headed up to 1984. Soon he resigned, going forward to head the Rembrandt Company. In 1988, he formed the Compagnie Financière Richemont, a Swiss luxury company that he currently chairs. In several other businesses, he still held executive positions.  During his career, Mr. Rupert has received numerous honors and accomplishments. He is the most powerful figure in South Africa three times. He has also been named Business Leader of the Year on many occasions and was honored by the World Economic Forum in 1992 as one of its Global Leaders of Tomorrow. He retains honorary doctorates from Stellenbosch University, Metropolitan University of Nelson Mandela, and St. Andrews University. In 2009, he won the University of Witwatersrand’s Management Excellence Award and was elected Chancellor of the University of Stellenbosch. In the same year, the French President named him Officer of the Hon Legion’s French National Order “We are living on the cusp of tremendous systemic change, and while many things may be in flux, one thing is clear: it’s time to give back and to put the human being back in the center of our systems,” he said. Mr. Rupert has worked successfully to promote a healthy society and a better world, one of his many achievements in leadership and industry, and he has been responsible for building a number of organizations that represent this commitment. He is already a founding member of the Small Business Development Corporation, now recognized as Business Partners Ltd, which since its establishment has generated more than half a million jobs in South Africa. He is also a member of the Nelson Mandela Children’s Fund Advisory Board and has been the Peace Parks Foundation’s Chairman since 2006. Mr. Rupert has also made a tremendous commitment to nurturing values and society’s increased value of sport. He was instrumental in establishing the Laureus Sport for Good Foundation, which significantly improves the power of sport to change the lives of underprivileged youth around the world, and co-founded the University of Cape Town’s Sports Science Institute.
